Marking Scheme

Understand the Scoring System

JEE Mains has two types of questions with different marking rules. Know them well.

✅ +4 Correct MCQ
Full 4 marks for every correct answer in both Section A and B
❌ −1 Wrong MCQ
1 mark deducted for wrong MCQ in Section A only. Think before marking!
🔢 +4 Correct Integer
Section B integer type questions — +4 for correct numerical answer
⭕ 0 Wrong Integer
No negative marking for integer type! Always attempt all integer questions
⏭️ 0 Unattempted
No marks deducted for skipped questions. Skip if unsure about MCQ
🏆 300 Maximum Marks
90 questions × 4 marks = 300 total. Target 200+ for a good NIT seat

Preparation Strategy

How to Crack JEE Mains

Proven strategies from students who cracked JEE Mains with 200+ marks.

1
Start with High-Weightage
Don't study all chapters equally. Physics: Electrostatics, Modern Physics, Optics first. These 3 alone contribute 30–40% of marks.
2
Never Skip Integer Type
Integer type has NO negative marking. Always attempt all 10 integer questions in each subject. Even a guess gives you +4 with zero risk.
3
NCERT Chemistry = 40 Marks
JEE Mains Chemistry is 70% NCERT. Read every line — reactions, properties, exceptions. 40 marks are almost free if you do this.
4
Chapter Test Before Mock
Don't jump to full mocks directly. Complete all chapter tests first — build subject by subject. Mock performance will be consistent.
5
Solve PYQ Last 10 Years
JEE Mains repeats concepts in similar format. Solving 10 years of PYQs trains your brain for the exact type of questions NTA asks.
6
Time Management in Exam
Start with your strongest subject. Spend max 2 min per question. Skip and come back. Never spend 10 min on one question.
