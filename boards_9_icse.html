ICSE Class 9 — Official 2026-27 Syllabus

Based on official CISCE 2026-27 PDFs from cisce.org. All 7 core subjects fully updated including the newly added Electricity & Magnetism in Physics.

Ch.1 · Pure Arithmetic
Rational and Irrational Numbers
Real numbers, surds, rationalisation of surds, simplifying by rationalising denominator, representation on number line, proofs of irrationality of √2, √3, √5
Important
Ch.2 · Commercial Maths
Compound Interest
CI as repeated SI with growing principal, amount formula A=P(1+r/100)^n, half-yearly compounding, finding A/P/r/n given combinations, CI–SI difference, rate of growth and depreciation
Important
Ch.3 · Algebra
Expansions and Factorisation
Algebraic identities: (a±b)², (a±b)³, (x±a)(x±b), (a±b±c)²; Factorisation: a²-b², a³±b³, ax²+bx+c by splitting middle term
Important
Ch.4 · Algebra
Simultaneous Linear Equations
Solving by elimination, substitution and cross-multiplication; framing and solving word problems
Important
Ch.5 · Algebra
Indices / Exponents
Positive, fractional, negative and zero indices; laws of indices: aᵐ×aⁿ=aᵐ⁺ⁿ, aᵐ÷aⁿ=aᵐ⁻ⁿ, (aᵐ)ⁿ=aᵐⁿ; simplification of expressions
Ch.6 · Algebra
Logarithms
Logarithmic vs exponential form, laws of logarithms, expansion of expressions using log laws (e.g. log y = 4log a + 2log b - 3log c)
Important
Ch.7 · Geometry
Triangles
Congruency (SSS, SAS, AAS, RHS); angles opposite equal sides; triangle inequalities; Mid-Point Theorem with proof; Equal Intercept Theorem; Pythagoras Theorem (area-based proof) and converse
Important
Ch.8 · Geometry
Rectilinear Figures
Parallelogram theorems (all 6 properties with proofs), constructions of quadrilaterals and regular hexagon, area theorems on parallelograms (same base and between same parallels)
Important
Ch.9 · Geometry
Circle — Chord Properties
Perpendicular from centre bisects chord, equal chords equidistant from centre, one circle through 3 points; arc and chord properties: equal arcs ↔ equal angles and equal chords
Important
Ch.10 · Statistics
Statistics
Raw, arrayed and grouped data, tally marks, mean and median of ungrouped data, class intervals, frequency tables, grouped frequency distributions (continuous intervals), frequency polygon
Ch.11 · Mensuration
Area, Perimeter and Volumes
Area and perimeter of triangles (Heron's), all quadrilaterals; circle area and circumference; sectors; surface area and volume of cube and cuboid (including inner/outer dimensions, cost, open/closed)
Important
Ch.12 · Trigonometry
Trigonometry
sin, cos, tan and reciprocals; standard angles 0°,30°,45°,60°,90°; simple 2D problems with right-angled triangle; complementary angle relations
Important
Ch.13 · Coordinate Geometry
Coordinate Geometry
Cartesian plane, ordered pairs, plotting points, solving simultaneous equations graphically, distance formula
Unit 1
Measurements and Experimentation
SI units, Vernier callipers (LC, zero error), micrometer screw gauge for length, simple pendulum for time — T=2π√(l/g), graph of l vs T²
Unit 2
Motion in One Dimension
Scalars and vectors, distance vs displacement, speed vs velocity, acceleration, distance-time and velocity-time graphs. Derived equations: v=u+at, s=ut+½at², v²=u²+2as, s=½(u+v)t
Important
Unit 3
Laws of Motion
Contact and non-contact forces, Newton's 3 laws, inertia, mass, momentum p=mv, F=ma, Gravitation: Universal Law, g, mass vs weight, free fall, gravitational units
Important
Unit 4
Fluids — Pressure and Buoyancy
Thrust and pressure, p=hρg, Pascal's law, atmospheric pressure, buoyancy, Archimedes' Principle, Relative Density, floatation principle (ρ₁/ρ₂ = V₂/V₁)
Important
Unit 5
Heat and Energy
Heat vs temperature, anomalous expansion of water (0-10°C), Hope's experiment, energy flow and laws of thermodynamics, renewable vs non-renewable energy sources, Global Warming and Greenhouse Effect — meaning, causes, impact
GH Effect Updated
Unit 6
Light — Reflection and Mirrors
Laws of reflection, plane mirrors (parallel and perpendicular), spherical mirrors (concave/convex), focal length f=R/2, ray diagrams for all positions, mirror formula (no derivation), uses of mirrors
Important
Unit 7
Sound
Nature and propagation of sound, V=fλ, effect of temperature and medium on speed, infrasonic/sonic/ultrasonic, applications of ultrasound, difference between ultrasonic and supersonic
Unit 8 · NEW ✨
Electricity and Magnetism
Electric cell, current as rate of charge flow, potential difference V=W/q, open and closed circuits, insulators and conductors. Induced magnetism, magnetic field of earth, neutral points, magnetic field lines, electromagnets. New unit added in 2026-27

Ch.1
The Language of Chemistry
Chemical symbols (IUPAC), formulae of common compounds, valency, balancing chemical equations, word equations and formula equations
Important
Ch.2
Chemical Changes and Reactions
Physical vs chemical changes, types of reactions (combination, decomposition, displacement, double displacement), exothermic and endothermic
Ch.3
Water
Hard and soft water, temporary and permanent hardness, methods of softening, water as a solvent, heavy water (D₂O)
Ch.4
Atomic Structure and Chemical Bonding
Bohr's model, electronic configuration, ionic bonding (electron transfer), covalent bonding (electron sharing), properties of ionic vs covalent compounds, Lewis dot structures
Important
Ch.5
The Periodic Table
Historical development (Dobereiner, Newlands, Mendeleev), Modern Periodic Table, periodicity: atomic radius, ionisation energy, electronegativity, metallic character
Important
Ch.6
Study of the First Element — Hydrogen
Position in periodic table, preparation (Bosch process, electrolysis), physical and chemical properties, reducing nature, uses, isotopes of hydrogen
Ch.7
Atmospheric Pollution
Air pollution — causes (burning fuels, industries), pollutants (CO, SO₂, NO₂, particulates), effects on health and environment, greenhouse gases, ozone layer depletion, acid rain, control measures
Important
Unit 1
Cell — The Basic Unit of Life
Cell as structural and functional unit, prokaryotic vs eukaryotic, plant vs animal cell, organelles (nucleus, mitochondria, chloroplast, ER, Golgi, vacuoles, plasma membrane, cell wall), osmosis, permeability
Important
Unit 2
Tissues — Plant and Animal
Meristematic tissues (apical, lateral, intercalary), permanent tissues (parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma, xylem, phloem), animal tissues (epithelial, connective, muscular — striated/smooth/cardiac, nervous)
Important
Unit 3
The Flower
Parts of a flower and their functions, types of flowers (complete/incomplete, bisexual/unisexual), inflorescence, floral formula basics
Unit 4
Pollination and Fertilisation
Self vs cross pollination, agents (wind, water, insects, animals), adaptations, fertilisation in flowering plants, double fertilisation, development of seed and fruit
Important
Unit 5
Seeds — Structure and Germination
Structure of monocot and dicot seeds, conditions for germination (water, air, temperature), types (epigeal, hypogeal), role of cotyledons, seed dispersal mechanisms
Unit 6
Respiration in Plants
Aerobic and anaerobic respiration, gaseous exchange in plants (stomata, lenticels), comparison of respiration and photosynthesis, Kreb's cycle (overview), fermentation
Important
Unit 7
Economic Importance of Bacteria and Fungi
Useful bacteria (nitrogen fixation, decomposition, dairy, medicines), harmful bacteria (diseases). Useful fungi (yeast in baking/brewing, antibiotics), harmful fungi (diseases, food spoilage)

Reading comprehension of ~500-word passage: word meanings, antonyms from passage, 5 questions for inference, 1 language question (e.g. voice transformation)

💡 Expert Tips

Score High in ICSE Class 9 — 2026-27

Tips based on the actual 2026-27 CISCE syllabus and exam pattern.

⚡
Physics: New Electricity Unit
Electricity & Magnetism is a completely new unit in 2026-27 ICSE Physics. It covers circuits, current, potential difference, induced magnetism and electromagnets. Start this early — it'll be fresh for examiners.
📐
Maths: Logs + CI are Unique to ICSE
Logarithms and Compound Interest are present in ICSE but not in CBSE Class 9. These carry high marks. Master the log laws and CI formula A=P(1+r/100)ⁿ thoroughly.
🌱
Biology: Draw Diagrams
ICSE Biology is heavily diagram-based. Cell organelles, plant tissues, flower parts, seed structure — practice neat labelled diagrams for all chapters. Poor diagrams = lost marks.
🌍
Geography: Focus on Chapters 1–5
Earth as a Planet, Rotation/Revolution, and Structure of the Earth carry the most marks. Understand equinoxes, solstices, tides and rock types with diagrams.
✍️
English: Vocabulary from Text
The comprehension asks for antonyms "from the passage." Read the passage twice before answering. Vocabulary questions require you to find words from the passage itself — not general vocabulary.
🔄
ICSE Depth > CBSE Breadth
ICSE tests deeper understanding than CBSE. Don't just memorise — understand WHY. In Maths, know proofs of all geometry theorems. In History, understand causes and effects, not just dates.
