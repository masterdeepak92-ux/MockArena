Why does Ampère's Law fail to give the magnetic field for a single rotating charged ring, even though it always holds in principle?
Ampère's Law always holds — the issue is symmetry, not the law itself. For a single ring, the magnetic field is not uniform along any circular Amperian loop you can draw, so you cannot factor B out of the line integral…

Why does anti-Markovnikov addition work with HBr + peroxides, but NOT with HCl or HI under the same conditions?
Only HBr has the right radical reactivity — it's a Goldilocks situation. HCl's Cl• radical is too reactive and adds non-selectively. HI's I• radical is too unreactive — the chain propagation step is endothermic…
