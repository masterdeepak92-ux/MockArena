About the Exam

What Makes JEE Advanced Different?

JEE Advanced is not harder JEE Mains. It's a fundamentally different exam — testing depth of understanding, multi-concept integration and analytical thinking.

🏆 Why JEE Advanced is Unique
Only top 2.5 lakh JEE Mains qualifiers can appear — you're already in the top 2% of India
Questions combine 2-3 concepts from different chapters — no single-concept problems
Multiple correct MCQs with complex partial marking — wrong guessing destroys your score
Conducted by IITs themselves (rotating) — IIT Madras conducts 2026
Both Paper 1 AND Paper 2 mandatory — full 6-hour exam day
No fixed syllabus revision each year — topics can be added by conducting IIT

Question Types

Know Every Question Format

JEE Advanced has 5 different question types across Paper 1 & 2. Each has different marking. Knowing these is half the battle.

☑️
Type 1
Single Correct MCQ
+3−1
One correct option out of four. Standard MCQ but JEE Advanced difficulty — multi-concept traps in every option.
Appears in: Both Paper 1 & 2
☑️☑️
Type 2 — Most Dangerous
Multiple Correct MCQ
+4 full+1 to +3 partial−2 wrong
One or MORE options can be correct. Partial marks if some correct chosen. −2 if any wrong selected. Most students lose marks here.
Most challenging section — never guess!
🔢
Type 3
Integer Type (0–9)
+30 wrong
Answer is a non-negative integer from 0 to 9. No negative marking — always attempt! Great marks opportunity.
Answer range: 0, 1, 2 ... 9
🗂️
Type 4
Matrix Match
+30 wrong
Match items in Column I with Column II. Each correct match gives marks. No negative marking. Tests systematic understanding.
Appears in: Paper 2 primarily
📖
Type 5
Paragraph Based
+3−1
A paragraph/passage is given followed by 2-3 linked questions. Tests reading speed + deep comprehension under time pressure.
Read passage carefully before answering

Advanced Strategy

How to Crack JEE Advanced

1
Multi-Correct = Make or Break
Never guess multi-correct. −2 for any wrong choice. Only mark when 100% sure of ALL correct options. Skip if even 1% doubt.
2
Integer Type = Free Marks
0 to 9, no negative marking. Even a calculated guess is +3 or 0 — never negative. Always attempt every single integer question.
3
Depth Over Breadth
JEE Advanced rewards depth. Master 5 topics completely rather than knowing 15 topics superficially. The questions probe corners.
4
First Clear JEE Mains Concepts
JEE Advanced builds on JEE Mains. All 13 Physics chapter tests here must score 60%+ before you attempt Advanced level problems.
5
Solve PYQ 2010–2024
IIT question setters have patterns. Solving 14 years of PYQ exposes recurring concept combinations that will appear again in 2026.
6
Both Papers are Equal
Paper 2 is often harder and students are tired. Practice full 6-hour sessions to build mental stamina — it's a marathon not a sprint.
