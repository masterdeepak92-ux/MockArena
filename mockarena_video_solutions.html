0:00Welcome to this comprehensive solution of the JEE Advanced 2023 question on rotating charged rings. This is one of the most conceptually rich problems in electromagnetism.
0:28Let's first state the problem precisely. We have a non-conducting ring of radius R carrying total charge Q, uniformly distributed, rotating with angular velocity ω.
1:05The first and most important step is to realise that this rotating charge creates an effective current. Think about it — charge is moving, and moving charge is current by definition.
1:52I_eff = Q × (ω / 2π) = Q × frequency
2:25Many students make the mistake here of writing I = Qω instead of Qω/2π. Remember — ω is in radians per second, and one full revolution is 2π radians. So the frequency is ω/2π.
3:20Now we need to find the magnetic field at the centre. The key question is: which law do we use — Biot-Savart or Ampère's Law?
7:08At the centre of the ring, every current element dl is at the same distance R from the centre. And critically, the angle between dl and the position vector r is exactly 90 degrees everywhere.
7:45dB = (μ₀/4π) × I × dl / R²
8:10Since sin θ equals 1 everywhere, we can integrate around the entire ring. The total circumference is 2πR, and the field at the centre is simply μ₀I divided by 2R.
9:00B = μ₀ × I_eff / (2R) = μ₀Qω / (4πR)
9:50Now let me explain why Ampère's Law cannot give you this answer numerically, even though it always holds in principle.
10:40For Ampère's Law to let you calculate B directly, you need the field to be constant in magnitude and direction along your entire Amperian loop. A single ring does not provide that symmetry.
12:40Now for the magnetic moment. This is where students often lose 2 marks unnecessarily. The formula is μ = I × A, where A is the area enclosed by the current loop.
13:30μ = I_eff × πR² = (Qω/2π) × πR² = QωR²/2
14:30And notice what happens when you take the ratio of μ to L, the angular momentum. This gives you the gyromagnetic ratio γ = Q/2m.
17:55This is the classical gyromagnetic ratio, and it appears in quantum mechanics too when we talk about orbital magnetic moments of electrons. JEE Advanced 2023 tested exactly this connection.
21:30Let me now give you the three exam tricks that can save you at least 2 minutes on this type of question in the actual exam.
1:52
I_eff formula: Always I = Qω/2π, NOT Qω. The /2π converts from radians/sec to revolutions/sec (actual frequency).
7:08
Biot-Savart centre trick: At centre, every element is equidistant (R) AND perpendicular (θ=90°) → sin θ = 1 → integral simplifies to just circumference × dB formula.
9:50
Ampère limitation: Works calculationally ONLY for infinite solenoid, infinite wire, toroid — NOT for finite objects like a single ring.
