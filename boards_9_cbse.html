🆕 Syllabus Updates

What Changed in CBSE Class 9 — 2026-27

CBSE has released a significantly revised Class 9 curriculum for 2026-27, aligned with NCF-SE 2023 and NEP 2020. Here are the major changes compared to last year.

📋 Key Changes from 2025-26 → 2026-27
Mathematics
New Chapter Added: Sequences and Progressions (AP & GP) — completely new in 2026-27. Unit marks revised: Geometry now 25M (was 27M), Statistics+Probability now 10M together.
Mathematics
Restructured: "Introduction to Polynomials" now includes linear equations and graphs as one chapter. Brahmagupta's formula added in Mensuration. Distance formula added in Coordinate Geometry.
Science
New Chapter Added: Earth as a System (Unit IV · 5M) — biogeochemical cycles, solar energy, differential heating of Earth. Completely new in 2026-27.
Science
New Chapter Added: Reproduction (Unit I) — sexual and asexual reproduction in plants and humans. Previously not in Class 9. Diversity (Classification) also added as new chapter.
Science
Restructured: Unit I is now "World of Living" (27M) — Cell, Tissues, Reproduction, Diversity. Unit II is "Matter" (25M). Unit III is "Motion, Force, Work, Sound" (23M).
Science
Removed: Gravitation is NO LONGER a separate chapter in 2026-27. It is merged within Force & Laws of Motion. "Improvement in Food Resources" renamed to "Food Production".
Science — Physics
New Topics: Simple Machines (pulley, inclined plane, lever) added to Work & Energy chapter. Echolocation added to Sound chapter.
Maths — Probability
Now Summative: Probability (Introduction to Probability) is now part of the annual exam in 2026-27 — it was formative-only in 2025-26.

CBSE Class 9 — All Chapters 2026-27

Based on official CBSE 2026-27 PDFs from cbseacademic.nic.in. Gold-bordered cards are new chapters added in 2026-27.

Unit I · 7 Marks
Number System
Rational numbers, representation on number line, density of rationals, decimal representation, irrational numbers, proof of irrationality of √2 and √3, square root spiral
Important
Unit II · Algebra
Introduction to Polynomials
Algebraic expressions, definition of polynomial, degree, linear polynomials, linear growth and decay, slope and y-intercept, graphing linear equations in two variables
Important
Unit II · Algebra · NEW ✨
Sequences and Progressions
Introduction to sequences, explicit and recursive rules, Arithmetic Progressions (AP) — nth term, sum, graphs. Geometric Progressions (GP) — nth term, fractals, Tower of Hanoi puzzle. New chapter added in 2026-27
New 2026-27Important
Unit II · Algebra
Exploring Algebraic Identities
Algebraic identities, geometric visualisation, factorisation using identities, quadratic expressions using algebra tiles, Remainder Theorem, Factor Theorem, simplifying rational expressions, Brahmagupta's quadratic formula
Important
Unit II · Algebra · 20M total
Linear Equations in Two Variables
Linear equations, graphical representation, slope-intercept form, pair of linear equations, graphical and algebraic methods (substitution, elimination), consistency and inconsistency, real-life applications
Important
Unit III · 4 Marks
Coordinate Geometry
Brief history, 2D Cartesian coordinate system, coordinates, distance between two points, midpoint of a line segment — distance formula is new addition in 2026-27
Distance Formula New
Unit IV · Geometry
Introduction to Euclid's Geometry: Axioms and Postulates
History of geometry, Sulbasutras square construction, Euclid's definitions, axioms of measurement, 5 postulates, parallelism, role of definitions and axioms in proving results
Unit IV · Geometry
Lines and Angles
Rays and angles, measures of angles, intersecting lines, pairs of angles, linear pair theorem, vertically opposite angles, parallel lines — corresponding, alternate, interior angles, triangle angle properties
Important
Unit IV · Geometry
Triangles — Congruence Theorems
Practical applications, triangle rigidity, congruence (SAS, SSS, ASA, RHS, AAS), isosceles triangle properties, propositions and their converses, counter examples for SSA
Important
Unit IV · Geometry · 25M total
4-gons (Quadrilaterals)
Properties and characterisation of parallelograms, midpoint theorem and converse, medians concurrent (2:1 ratio), 4-gon formed by midpoints is parallelogram, reflection and rotation symmetries, tiling with 4-gons
Important
Unit IV · Geometry
Circles
Circle definitions, chords and angles subtended, perpendicular bisectors of chords, equidistant chords, angle at centre = double at remaining circle, angles in same segment, cyclic quadrilateral (supplementary opposite angles)
Important
Unit V · Mensuration
Area and Perimeter
Perimeter of shapes, circumference, introduction to Pi (irrationality), length of arc, area of shapes (rectangles, parallelograms, triangles), Heron's formula, area of circle, sector, Brahmagupta's formula for cyclic quadrilateral — new in 2026-27
Brahmagupta NewImportant
Unit V · 14M total
Surface Area and Volume
Cuboid, cube, cylinder, cone, pyramid, sphere and hemisphere — surface area and volume. Note: Full set of solids now in 2026-27 (previously only sphere/cone were summative)
Important
Unit VI · Statistics
Statistics
Graphical representation of data (bar graphs, histograms, stacked bar graphs, 100% stacked bar graphs), measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode), weighted average
Unit VI · 10M total · NOW SUMMATIVE ✨
Introduction to Probability
Concept of randomness, probability scale, empirical probability, theoretical probability, sample space, events, tree diagrams and tables. Now in annual exam — was formative-only in 2025-26
Now in ExamImportant
Unit I · World of Living · 27M
Cell — The Basic Unit of Life
Discovery of cell, plant vs animal cell, prokaryotic vs eukaryotic, cell as structural and functional unit, organelles (nucleus, mitochondria, chloroplast, ER, vacuoles, plasma membrane, cell wall), permeability, cell division, osmosis
BiologyImportant
Unit I · World of Living
Tissues
Levels of organisation in living organisms, plant tissues (meristematic, permanent — parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma, xylem, phloem), animal tissues (epithelial, connective, muscular, nervous), musculoskeletal system basics
BiologyImportant
Unit I · World of Living · NEW ✨
Reproduction
Asexual reproduction (budding, spore formation), sexual reproduction in flowering plants (flower parts, pollination, fertilisation, seed dispersal), sexual reproduction in humans (male/female reproductive system, menstrual cycle, birth control). New chapter in 2026-27
New 2026-27Important
Unit I · World of Living · NEW ✨
Diversity (Classification)
Importance of classification, five kingdoms and key features, major divisions of plants and animals, binomial nomenclature, acellular entities (viruses). New chapter in 2026-27
New 2026-27

Homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures, solutions/suspensions/colloids and properties, concentration expression (mass by mass %, mass by volume %, volume by volume %), separation techniques: crystallisation, distillation, paper chromatography, sublimation, centrifugation, coagulation
Chemistry
Unit II · Matter
Atoms and Molecules
Law of conservation of mass, law of constant proportion, Dalton's atomic theory, molecules of elements and compounds, ions, ionic and covalent compounds, chemical formulae, molecular mass, formula unit mass, electron dot structures

Structure of the Atom
Subatomic particles, atomic models (Thomson, Rutherford, Bohr), electron distribution in shells (K,L,M,N — up to 18 elements), valency, atomic number, mass number, isotopes, isobars, IUPAC symbols
ChemistryImportant

Motion
Displacement, velocity, acceleration, position-time and velocity-time graphs (uniform & uniformly accelerated motion), kinematic equations derived by graphical method, speed for uniform circular motion

Force and motion, balanced and unbalanced forces, friction, Newton's 3 laws, SI unit of force. Note: Gravitation merged here in 2026-27 — mass vs weight, universal law of gravitation, acceleration due to gravity (g)

Work done, work-energy theorem, kinetic and potential energy, conservation of energy, power. New addition 2026-27: Simple machines — pulley, inclined plane, lever; mechanical advantage

Earth as interconnected system, solar radiation and electromagnetic spectrum, differential heating of Earth, winds (mountain/valley/sea/land breezes), biogeochemical cycles (water, carbon, nitrogen, oxygen), human impact. Completely new chapter 2026-27

💡 Expert Tips

Score High in Class 9 CBSE 2026-27

Tips specific to the 2026-27 revised syllabus — focus on the new chapters and revised units.

🆕
Start New Chapters Early
Sequences & Progressions (Maths), Reproduction, Diversity and Earth as a System (Science) are brand new in 2026-27. Most students will be less prepared — start these early for an advantage.
📐
Geometry = 25 Marks
Geometry is still the highest-scoring unit in Maths (25M). Master all theorems in Lines & Angles, Triangles, Quadrilaterals and Circles. Write proofs step-by-step in exams.
🎲
Probability is Now in Exam!
Introduction to Probability is now summative in 2026-27 (was formative-only last year). It carries marks in the annual exam. Practice tree diagrams, sample space and theoretical probability.
🔬
Science Unit I = 27 Marks
World of Living carries the most Science marks (27M). Covers 4 chapters — Cell, Tissues, Reproduction and Diversity. Don't skip Reproduction — it's new and examiners will ask from it.
🌍
Earth as a System = Easy 5 Marks
The new Earth as a System chapter (Unit IV, 5 marks) covers biogeochemical cycles and solar energy. It's conceptual and easy to score — don't ignore it thinking it's small.
🗺️
SST Map Work = 5 Marks
History has 2 map marks (France, WWI/WWII), Geography has 3 map marks (India rivers/mountains). Practice locating all required features on blank maps — these are guaranteed marks.
